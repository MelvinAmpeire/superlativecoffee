COFFEE; - UGANDA’S GREEN GOLD

by admin
April, 5

Along the equator in the Eastern part of Africa lies a country that nature has endowed with both high and low lands receiving rains in mainly two seasons separated by a dry spell in the different months of the year. From the slopes of Mt. Elgon in the far east of the country, through the central region to the western block mountain Rwenzori you will see green fields majority of them grown with coffee trees.

Here It all happens in twos; the types of coffee grown, Arabica which was introduced from Ethiopia by colonialists is found hanging in the high altitudes of the East and western parts of the country, while Robusta which is native in the Lake Victoria crescent is seen taking cover in on the lower lands of the central and western plains. The two harvest seasons with a major harvest and fly picking for both varieties, in tandem with the two rains seasons that the biggest part of the country experiences give us the red cherries that are processed in two main ways the natural and wet processing to give a coffee with unique taste attributes of balanced sweetness and acidity that awaits you in the cup.

From the farms where the trees are taken care of by about 2 million households, to marketing, transport and value addition, over 32% of Ugandans find themselves in one way or the other connecting with at least on part of the coffee value chain. This make coffee business not only the back born of the country’s economy but potentially one of the major employer in a country where over 75% are youth. Uganda is second to only Ethiopia when it comes to producing coffee on the African continent, but since almost all of the country’s production is exported, it ranks number one in Africa with supplies of over 6M bags of green coffee to the world to consume which puts it in the 8th position globally.
